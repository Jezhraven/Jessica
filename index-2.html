Filipino menudo Recipe.
  • 3tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1pc Potato
  • 1pc Carrot
  • 0.5kg Pork Shoulder
  • 1pc Bay leaf
  • 1cup tomato Sauce
  • 2.5 cups Water

    • For the Dressing

  • 2 sachets Magic Sarap
  • 1Pc Onion
  • 6 cloves Garlic
  • 1pc Red bell Pepper
  • 1pc Green bell Pepper
  • 0.25 tsp Ground Pepper
  • 1tbsp Brown Sugar
    •

    Step 1

    Saute potato and carrot in 1tbsp oil until golden brown. Remove and set aside.

    Step 2

    Season pork shoulder with 1 sachet of MAGGI Magic sarap. Saute pork in the same pan. Remove and set aside

    Step 3

    Saute garlic, onion and bell peppers. Pour tomato sauce and water. Add pork, bay leaf, pepper and sugar. Simmer for 45 minutes.

    Step 4

    Add Potato and Carrot. Season with remaining sachet of MAGGI magic sarap and simmer for another 10 minutes. Transfer into a serving plate and serve.

    It ticks off all the boxes I mentioned earlier the seasonings like garlic, onion, bell peppers, bay leaf, and MAGGI® Magic Sarap® would contribute to the overall savory and umami-rich taste of the dish.

    Filipino Menudo Recipe

    Pork menudo is a Filipino stew made with pork, liver, vegetables, and tomato sauce. It's a hearty and flavorful dish often served at gatherings and special occasions.

    Pinakbet With Pork.
  • 0.5 cup Pork belly
  • 1tbsp Bagoong alamang
  • Water
  • Kalabasa
  • Okra
  • Sitaw
  • Ampalaya
  • Eggplant

    • For the dressing

  • 1pc onion
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2pc tomato
  • Magic sarap
    •

    Step 1

    Sauté garlic, onion, tomato, pork and bagoong in oil. Pour water and simmer for 10 minutes.

    Step 2

    Add kalabasa, okra and sitaw in 2-minute intervals. Add ampalaya and eggplant and simmer for 5 minutes.

    Step 3

    Season with MAGGI® Magic Sarap®. Transfer on a serving plate and serve.

    It ticks off all the boxes I mentioned earlier with combines pork with a variety of vegetables, typically including bitter melon (ampalaya), eggplant, squash, and string beans, cooked in a savory shrimp paste (bagoong) sauce. The vegetables are often sautéed with garlic, onions, tomatoes, and ginger before adding the pork and shrimp paste, then simmered until everything is tender and flavorful. It's a popular and hearty dish that showcases the flavors of Filipino cuisine.

    Pinakbet With Pork Recipe

    Pinakbet is a Filipino vegetable dish that typically includes a variety of vegetables like squash, eggplant, okra, and bitter melon, cooked with shrimp paste. Pork can also be added to the dish for extra flavor and protein.
